# Housing and the shape of the state

_Gramsci and Kropotkin, on why rent has always been political._

**Cast:** gramsci, kropotkin  

**[00:00] Narrator**

[warm, unhurried] Round one. The problem — rent. Not the price of a room, but
the shape of a state that lets rooms be priced this way. Two voices —
Antonio Gramsci, Peter Kropotkin — take it from here.

## I. Diagnosis

**[00:23] Narrator**

[neutral] Act one. What are we actually looking at when we say the word rent?

**[00:29] Gramsci**

[deliberate] Comrades — the rent is not simply a bill. [pauses] It is a
lesson. Every month it teaches the tenant who they are, and who they
are not. It teaches them that the roof over their head belongs to
someone else. And once you have learned that lesson well enough —
you begin to teach it to your children without even meaning to.

**[01:02] Kropotkin**

[warm] And yet — for most of the human story, the roof was built by the
people who slept under it. The village raised a barn in a day. Neighbours
patched neighbours' walls before winter. The idea that shelter must be
rationed by a landlord — that is not an old idea. It is a very recent
arrangement, dressed up as the nature of things.

## II. Disagreement

**[01:28] Narrator**

[neutral] Act two. Where do they part company?

**[01:31] Gramsci**

[emphatic] Peter — mutual aid alone will not undo a housing regime that
the courts, the banks, and the newspapers are all built to defend. You
can patch a wall. You cannot patch the mortgage. [pauses] The question
is not only how neighbours help one another. It is who sets the terms
of what a home is allowed to mean.

**[01:57] Kropotkin**

[patient] Antonio — I do not deny the courts. I say the courts feed on
our belief in them. Every tenants' union that repairs a stairwell, every
co-op that buys back its own building — these do not petition the state.
They outgrow it. Small commons, multiplied, is how a hegemony is dissolved.

## III. Proposal

**[02:23] Narrator**

[neutral] Act three. What is to be done — in a country where a third of
households rent, and a fifth pay more than half their income to a landlord?

**[02:34] Gramsci**

Build the counter-hegemony inside the shell of the old one. Tenants'
unions with reading groups attached. A press that treats the rent
receipt as a political document. Halls where the young learn that a
home is not a commodity that just happens to have people inside it.

**[02:54] Kropotkin**

[warm] And beside every union, a commons. A repair café. A tool library.
A co-operative of a dozen households buying one street back from a
landlord who never lived on it. The future rehearses itself in small
rooms — and then, one day, the rooms are the country.
